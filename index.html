          Designer Bio Editorial Guide

          Standards and conventions for writing mini biographies in the Google Fonts designers catalog

          Contents

          1. Overview
          2. Bio Structure
          3. Voice and Perspective
          4. Writing for Individual Designers
          5. Writing for Studios and Foundries
          6. Length Guidelines
          7. What to Include
          8. What to Avoid
          9. Link Formatting
          10. HTML Format Reference
          11. Examples
          12. Pre-Submission Checklist

          1. Overview

          Each designer in the Google Fonts catalog has a profile page with a short biography. These bios serve as the public-facing introduction to the people and organizations behind the typefaces in the library. They should be concise, accurate, and professional.

          Bios are stored as HTML in the bio.html file within each designer's directory in the google/fonts repository at catalog/designers/{designer_id}/bio.html.

          2. Bio Structure

          Every bio follows a consistent structure:

          Paragraph 1 Identity and core description — who they are, where they are based, what they do
          Paragraph 2 optional Additional context — notable work, career highlights, education, or specializations
          Final Paragraph Links — website, social media, and portfolio links separated by pipes ( | )

          3. Voice and Perspective

          Do
          • Write in third person ("Alice Savoie is a type designer...")
          • Use present tense for current roles and activities
          • Use past tense for completed education, past positions, and historical events
          • Maintain a neutral, encyclopedic tone — factual and respectful
          Don't
          • Use first person ("I am a designer...")
          • Use second person ("You'll love their fonts...")
          • Use promotional or marketing language ("award-winning genius")
          • Editorialize or offer personal opinions about the designer's work

          4. Writing for Individual Designers

          For individual type designers, the opening sentence should follow this pattern:

          <p>{Full Name} is a {role/title} based in {City, Country}.</p>

          If the location is unknown, omit the "based in" clause. If the designer has multiple roles, list the most relevant ones:

          Good

          "Alexei Vanyashin is a type designer and Cyrillic specialist dedicated to thoughtful, collaborative work."

          Good

          "Adam Jagosz is a Polish type designer and frontend developer based in Bielsko-Biała."

          Avoid

          "Adam is a cool designer who makes awesome fonts." (too casual, first name only, subjective)

          After the opening, provide 1–3 sentences of context. Prioritize in this order:

          1. Notable typefaces in the Google Fonts library
          2. Professional background relevant to type design
          3. Education in type design or related fields
          4. Foundry affiliation or studio
          5. Awards and recognitions (stated factually, not promotionally)

          5. Writing for Studios and Foundries

          For organizations, the opening should identify what the entity is, where it is located, and who founded it:

          <p>Based in {City}, {Studio Name} is a {type of organization} founded by {Founder Name}.</p>
          Good

          "Based in Tel Aviv, AlefAlefAlef is a leading Hebrew type foundry and design collective founded by Avraham Cornfeld."

          Good

          "Based in Lagos, Nigeria, Afrotype, founded by Seyi Olusanya, intends to subvert cliches around African graphic design and identity by creating typefaces that are grounded in African history and culture."

          For companies with a broader mission statement (beyond type), keep it brief and focused on their type-related work.

          6. Length Guidelines

          Minimum 1 sentence Name, role, and location. Acceptable when no further information is available.
          Ideal 2–4 sentences Identity + notable work or background. Covers the essentials without being exhaustive.
          Maximum ~150 words For designers with extensive, notable careers. Should still be concise and skimmable.

          The links paragraph at the end does not count toward the word limit.

          7. What to Include

          Element Priority Notes
          Full name Required As it appears in the designer directory. Use proper diacritics.
          Professional role Required "type designer", "graphic designer", "type foundry", etc.
          Location (city, country) Recommended Where they are currently based. Omit if unknown.
          Notable typefaces Recommended Especially those available on Google Fonts.
          Foundry or studio Recommended If they founded or are affiliated with one.
          Education Optional Relevant type design or design programs only.
          Awards Optional State factually: "recipient of the Prix Charles Peignot (2013)".
          Script specialization Optional Cyrillic, Arabic, Devanagari, etc. Highly relevant for multi-script designers.
          Website / portfolio link Required At least one link when available. Placed in the final paragraph.

          8. What to Avoid

          The following elements must never appear in designer biographies. These policies ensure consistency, professionalism, and accuracy across the catalog.

          Never include
          • Arctic Code Vault or any GitHub badge/recognition — not relevant to professional profiles
          • Personal contact information (email addresses, phone numbers)
          • Subjective superlatives ("the best", "world-renowned", "legendary")
          • Pricing or commercial information about font sales
          • Unverified claims or information that cannot be corroborated
          • Lengthy project descriptions — keep to typeface names, not full project narratives
          • Broken or placeholder URLs (e.g., https://This, https://one:)
          • Content copied verbatim from external sources without verification

          9. Link Formatting

          Links go in the final paragraph of the bio, separated by pipes with spaces:

          <p><a href="https://example.com">example.com</a> | <a href="https://instagram.com/user">Instagram</a></p>
          Conventions
          • Use the platform name as link text for social media: "Instagram", "LinkedIn", "Behance"
          • Use the domain name as link text for personal websites: "example.com", "foundryname.co"
          • All links must use HTTPS where available
          • Add target="_blank" attribute for external links
          • Verify all links are working before submission
          • Prefer permanent, canonical URLs (not shortened or tracking links)

          Preferred link order: Personal website, then social profiles alphabetically (Behance, GitHub, Instagram, LinkedIn, X/Twitter).

          10. HTML Format Reference

          Bios are stored as HTML fragments (not full documents). The only tags used are:

          Tag Usage
          <p> Wraps each paragraph. Every bio must use <p> tags.
          <a href="..."> Hyperlinks. Used in the final paragraph for website/social links.

          Do not use <b>, <i>, <br>, <div>, <span>, or any other HTML tags. Keep the markup minimal.

          Template: 
          <p>{Full Name} is a {role} based in {City, Country}. {Additional context}.</p>
<p><a href="https://website.com" target="_blank">website.com</a> | <a href="https://instagram.com/handle" target="_blank">Instagram</a></p>

          Quality Assessment

          Based on this random sample of 30 out of 364 existing bios (8.2%), the pre-existing catalog is in poor compliance with the Editorial Guide. Only 1 bio (Artur Schmal) fully passes the checklist. The most pervasive issue — missing target="_blank" on links — affects nearly three-quarters of all bios. Beyond this mechanical issue, a third of bios use first names only in their opening sentences, and another third contain promotional or marketing language that conflicts with the guide's neutral, encyclopedic tone.

          The HTML quality is generally acceptable, but there are cases of malformed markup (unclosed <p> tags, missing attribute quotes). Several bios include links using HTTP instead of HTTPS even though the destinations support HTTPS.

          Structurally, many bios were clearly written before any editorial standard existed: they use casual tone, first-person voice, vague filler phrases, or read more like marketing copy than encyclopedia entries. This suggests that a systematic cleanup of existing bios would be a worthwhile project, starting with the most clearly fixable issues (target="_blank", HTTPS, broken HTML) and then progressing to tone and content rewrites.

          Detailed Non-Compliance Reports

          Below are detailed reports for 8 representative non-compliant bios, chosen to illustrate the diversity of issues found.

          1. NORD ID (nordid)

          “Based in Stockholm & Copenhagen, NORD ID is a leading strategy & design agency creating coherent, distinct and differentiating brand identities. Uniquely combining insightful conceptual design with unparalleled craft, more often developing custom type design to be a key brand building component to create instant recognition across any and all touchpoints: From custom typefaces for large companies such as Vattenfall, Klarna and Skanska, down to more experimental type design for campaigns such as Chillboards for Coors.”

          Issues found:

          • Promotional language: “leading”, “Uniquely combining insightful conceptual design with unparalleled craft” — violates the “no subjective superlatives or marketing language” rule
          • Link text: Says “website” instead of the domain name “nordid.se”
          • Missing target="_blank" on the link

          Suggested fix: Rewrite to remove promotional language. Open with “Based in Stockholm and Copenhagen, NORD ID is a strategy and design agency.” Focus on factual output (custom typefaces for named clients). Fix link text to “nordid.se” and add target="_blank".

          2. Vernon Adams (vernonadams)

          “Vernon practiced typeface design professionally from 2007 to 2014. A lifelong artist, during this period he eagerly explored designing type for the mobile/cloud era of computing. His work spans all genres, from lively script faces to workhorse text families and operating system UI. Vernon graduated with an MA in Typeface Design from the University of Reading (UK) in 2006, and passed away in California in 2014. Almost all his typefaces live on as open source Google Fonts, and his favorite projects were Oxygen Mono, Monda, and Bowlby One. Follow his story at sansoxygen.com”

          Issues found:

          • First name only: Opens with “Vernon practiced” instead of “Vernon Adams”
          • Link embedded in body: The link to sansoxygen.com is inside the main paragraph, not in a separate final links paragraph
          • HTTP link: Uses http://sansoxygen.com instead of HTTPS
          • Missing target="_blank"
          • Editorializing: “A lifelong artist”, “eagerly explored”, “lively script faces”

          Suggested fix: Open with “Vernon Adams was a type designer” (past tense appropriate since he passed away). Move link to a separate final paragraph with HTTPS and proper formatting. Remove editorial language; state his work factually. Name his Google Fonts contributions explicitly.

          3. IBM BX&D (ibmbxd)

          “IBM Brand Experience & Design (BX&D) oversees the expression and strategic evolution of every IBM brand and sub-brand worldwide. Our work crosses research and strategy, communications and content development, symbology and systems, digital and physical experiences, tools and training. Ultimately, BX&D is here to make sure the role of the IBM brand is well understood and well represented—across the globe and across every experience.”

          Issues found:

          • First-person voice: “Our work crosses...” and “BX&D is here to make sure” — violates the third-person requirement
          • Link text: Says “Homepage” instead of domain “ibm.com/design”
          • No mention of typefaces: IBM Plex (their major Google Fonts contribution) is not mentioned

          Suggested fix: Rewrite entirely in third person following the foundry/studio template. Mention IBM Plex as their contribution to Google Fonts. Example opening: “IBM Brand Experience & Design (BX&D) is the design division of IBM responsible for the company’s brand identity worldwide.”

          4. Carolina Short (carolinashort)

          “Carolina is a designer from Buenos Aires. After 30 years of working in the industry, and part-time teaching experience in Argentina, Germany and New Zealand, took a full-time academic position at the University of Waikato. Her education was in traditional graphic design, with a strong emphasis on typography, which became her deep interest. In 1994 she created the experimental typeface Miyuscules, one of the first Argentine digital fonts. In 1996 became a digital nomad, founded Bigital with Tomás García Ferrari, and started developing projects for clients in different countries...”

          Issues found:

          • First name only: “Carolina is a designer” instead of “Carolina Short”
          • Broken HTML: Missing </p> closing tag (only one <p> opened for the body, never closed before the links paragraph)
          • Grammar errors: “took a full-time academic position” and “In 1996 became a digital nomad” — both missing the subject “she”
          • HTTP link: http://bigital.com/ (redirects to HTTPS)
          • Vague filler: “keeps thinking about new typography projects, currently with other fonts under development”

          Suggested fix: Open with “Carolina Short is a designer and educator from Buenos Aires, Argentina.” Fix grammar (add missing subjects). Close the <p> tag. Use HTTPS for links. Remove the vague ending and focus on Mansalva (her Google Fonts contribution).

          5. Hana Tanimura (hanatanimura)

          “Hana is an award-winning designer and art director at Google Creative Lab in New York (previously in London). She likes making good things with good people, for good causes. And sometimes just for fun.”

          Issues found:

          • First name only: “Hana is” instead of “Hana Tanimura is”
          • Promotional superlative: “award-winning” is a subjective superlative per the guide. If specific awards exist, they should be named factually.
          • Casual / editorial tone: “She likes making good things with good people, for good causes. And sometimes just for fun.” — this reads like a personal tagline, not an encyclopedic bio
          • Link text: “Homepage” instead of domain “hanatanimura.com”
          • No mention of typefaces: No reference to specific Google Fonts contributions

          Suggested fix: Open with “Hana Tanimura is a designer and art director at Google Creative Lab in New York.” Replace the tagline with factual information about her type design work. Fix link text to domain name. Name any specific typefaces she contributed to Google Fonts.

          6. Facundo Santana (facundosantana)

          “Facundo Santana, from Mar del Plata, Argentina, is the Co-founder & Managing Partner of basement.studio, where he leads growth, operations, and strategy. With a background in design and a sharp business acumen, Facundo bridges creativity and strategy to drive the company’s vision forward. In addition, he is a member of SoDA, an exclusive network of digital agency leaders, and the Entrepreneurs’ Organization (EO), an LP at Newtopia, and actively supports innovative startups as both an investor and advisor through basement ventures.”

          Issues found:

          • Pervasive promotional language: “sharp business acumen”, “bridges creativity and strategy to drive the company’s vision forward”, “exclusive network”, “actively supports innovative startups”
          • Not type-design focused: The entire bio is about business and startup activities; no typefaces or type design work is mentioned at all
          • Missing target="_blank" on link

          Suggested fix: Rewrite to focus on type design. Open with “Facundo Santana is a designer based in Mar del Plata, Argentina, and co-founder of basement.studio.” Remove all promotional language and business-focused content. Add mention of specific typefaces contributed to Google Fonts.

          7. Satsuyako (satsuyako)

          “A freelance designer based in Saitama, Japan. Started designing Japanese fonts in 2012, including Yomogi, Cherry Bomb One, and more. They have also worked on Latin fonts since 2019.”

          Issues found:

          • No full name: Opens with “A freelance designer” without any name. The guide requires the full name in the opening sentence.
          • Links in body paragraph: Font names (Yomogi, Cherry Bomb One) are hyperlinked to Google Fonts specimen pages inside the body paragraph, which mixes content links with the bio structure
          • Link text issues: Final paragraph uses “website” (lowercase) and “twitter” (lowercase) instead of domain name and proper platform name “Twitter”
          • Missing sentence subjects: “Started designing” lacks a subject

          Suggested fix: Open with “Satsuyako is a type designer based in Saitama, Japan.” Remove hyperlinks from font names in the body. Add proper subjects to sentences. Fix link text to “satsuyako.com” and “Twitter” (or “X”) with proper capitalization.

          8. Ross Mills (rossmills)

          “Ross Mills is a Canadian type designer and font maker, and co-founder of Tiro Typeworks (1994).”

          Issues found:

          • Broken HTML: The closing tag is <p> instead of </p>, resulting in malformed markup
          • No links paragraph: No website or social media links provided despite Tiro Typeworks having a well-known website (tiro.com)

          Suggested fix: Fix the closing tag to </p>. Add a links paragraph with the Tiro Typeworks website. Consider adding mention of notable Tiro typefaces on Google Fonts.

          Recommendations

          1. Quick mechanical fixes: A script could add target="_blank" to all links missing it, and upgrade HTTP to HTTPS. This would fix issues in 73% and 13% of bios respectively, with no editorial judgment required.
          2. Fix broken HTML: At least 3 bios have malformed HTML (unclosed tags, missing attribute quotes). These should be fixed as a priority since they may affect rendering.
          3. Full-name standardization: 10 bios (33%) use first names only. These should be updated to use the full name from the designer directory.
          4. Tone cleanup: Bios with promotional language, first-person voice, or casual tone need editorial rewriting. This is the most labor-intensive category and likely needs human review.
          5. Link text standardization: Update link text to use domain names for websites and platform names for social media, per the guide.

          Methodology

          30 bios were selected at random (using system random source) from the 364 existing bio.html files in the google/fonts catalog, excluding the 8 bios written by Claude in PR #10224. Each bio was checked against all 14 items in the Editorial Guide’s Pre-Submission Checklist. The sample represents 8.2% of the catalog.
